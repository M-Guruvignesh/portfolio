CHARACTERISTICS STUDY AND COMPARISION OF COVENTIONAL AND NON-CONVENTIONAL DRILLING EDM HOLE MAKING METHOD

ABSTRACT

Drilling processes play a vital role in various industries, including manufacturing, aerospace, automotive, and energy sectors. Over the years, both conventional and non-conventional drilling techniques have been employed to meet the increasing demands of precision and efficiency. This study aims to provide a comprehensive comparison between conventional and non-conventional drilling methods using Electrical Discharge Machining (EDM) as a non-conventional approach. It specifically focuses on the comparison between conventional and non-conventional drilling techniques, with a specific emphasis on the use of EDM. The study evaluates the fundamental principles and mechanisms involved in conventional drilling, which typically employs drill bits to remove material through rotational motion. It then explores the unique characteristics of non-conventional drilling techniques, such as EDM, which utilizes controlled electrical discharges to erode the workpiece. The comparison encompasses several key aspects, including material removal rates, surface finish quality, tool wear, heat-affected zones, and process flexibility. It analyzes the advantages and disadvantages associated with both conventional and non-conventional drilling methods, highlighting their suitability for different applications. The findings of this study contribute to advancing the knowledge of drilling processes and aid in the selection of the most suitable drilling technique for different industrial applications. Additionally, the insights gained from this comparative analysis can guide future research and development efforts towards enhancing drilling techniques and optimizing their performance.

Stainless steel Super Duplex 2507 is designed to handle highly corrosive conditions and situations where high strength is required. High molybdenum, chromium and nitrogen content in Super Duplex 2507 help the material withstand pitting and crevice corrosion. The material is also resistant to chloride stress corrosion cracking, to erosion corrosion, to corrosion fatigue, to general corrosion in acids. This alloy has good weldability and very high mechanical strength. Duplex 2507 is a super duplex stainless steel designed for applications which demand exceptional strength and corrosion resistance. Alloy 2507 has 25% chromium, 4% molybdenum, and 7% nickel. Extended elevated temperature exposure can reduce both the toughness and corrosion resistance of alloy 2507. Duplex 2507 possesses excellent mechanical properties. Often a light gauge of 2507 material can be used to achieve the same design strength of a thicker nickel alloy. Drill Bit

High-speed steel (HSS) drill bits are a type of tough, durable drill bit traditionally used for high-volume production drilling. Production drilling involves fast, repetitive drilling with few if any breaks between tasks. Many manufacturers exclusively produce drill bits that employ HSS construction because they are so versatile. HSS drill bits contain key elements such as carbon, and are often used to work on other types of metal including aluminium and other softer grades of steel. Many hand tools are made with machines that feature HSS cutting attachments and drill bits.

Copper Electrode

An electrode is a conductor through which electrical current enters or leaves a substance. In the context of Electrical Discharge Machining (EDM), an electrode is a tool used to create the desired shape or feature on the workpiece by removing material through a series of electric discharges.

EDM

The principle of working of EDM is based on the erosion of the material by repetitive spark between the workpiece and the tool submerged in a bath of the dielectric medium. The workpiece and tools are the two electrodes connected by D.C pulse generator. Electrode tool is shaped tool of required profile to cut on the workpiece and electrode are the separated by the gap to establish a pulsated spark across which the dielectric fluid flows. The servo drive system maintains the spark gap for continuous operation with desired feed of the tool.

CNC

CNC (Computer Numerical Control) machines are automated manufacturing tools that are controlled by computers and follow pre-programmed instructions to carry out precise and complex operations. The principles of CNC machines revolve around their ability to automate and control various aspects of the machining process.

Result and Conclusion

EDM shows lower material removal rate (MRR), but Surface roughness is better compared to conventional machining process. Unlike conventional processes, an EDM surface has a very random texture. The EDM cutting process there is no direct contact between the Electrode and the workpiece, which allows low tool wear compare to the conventional machining. Compare to the conventional, EDM drilling for micro holes without roughness. It creates good surface finish. Machining time also less than the conventional machining process. This is advantageous when directionality or “lay” could influence product performance. The surface finish of the parts produced by EDM usually consists of pits. However, by strictly controlling certain parameters, this process produces excellent mirror surface finish without polishing. It gives higher tool life, due to proper lubrication and cooling. We done a project comparing EDM and CNC machining. We used super duplex stainless steel and mild steel to compare both of the process, at last we conclude the stress, strain and displacement are low in the EDM process compare to CNC machining process. And the surface roughness is also better in EDM compare to conventional machining process. At last we conclude the EDM process have the better properties in all compare to CNC machining so EDM process is the best in future.
